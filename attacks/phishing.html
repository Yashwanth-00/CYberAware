Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted entities to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, financial details, or authentication tokens.

How Phishing Works (Attack Flow)

  • 1. Initial Contact: Attacker sends a spoofed email, SMS, or message.
  • 2. Social Engineering: Message creates urgency, fear, or trust.
  • 3. Malicious Redirection: Victim clicks a link to a cloned website.
  • 4. Credential Harvesting: Login data is captured and sent to attacker-controlled servers.
  • 5. Exploitation: Attacker performs account takeover or lateral movement.

Common Techniques Used

  • Domain Spoofing: Using similar-looking URLs to impersonate legitimate services.
  • Email Header Manipulation: Forging sender addresses to bypass filters.
  • Fake HTTPS Certificates: Making phishing pages appear secure.
  • Clone Websites: Replicating official login portals.

Impact on Security (CIA Model)

  • Confidentiality: Exposure of personal and organizational data.
  • Integrity: Fraudulent transactions or account modifications.
  • Availability: Locked or hijacked accounts.

Detection Indicators

  • Unexpected login alerts from unfamiliar locations.
  • Suspicious or misspelled domains.
  • Urgent language demanding immediate action.
  • Requests for credentials via email.

Mitigation & Defense

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Use DMARC, SPF, and DKIM email validation.
  • Deploy advanced email filtering systems.
  • Conduct regular security awareness training.
