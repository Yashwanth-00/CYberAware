Insider Threats

An insider threat occurs when a trusted individual within an organization — such as an employee, contractor, or partner — misuses their authorized access to compromise security. Unlike external attackers, insiders already have legitimate system access.

Types of Insider Threats

  • Malicious Insider: Intentionally steals or sabotages data for financial gain or revenge.
  • Negligent Insider: Causes accidental breaches due to poor security practices.
  • Compromised Insider: An employee whose credentials were stolen by an external attacker.

How Insider Threats Occur

  • Abuse of privileged access to sensitive databases.
  • Unauthorized copying of confidential files.
  • Bypassing internal access controls.
  • Installing unauthorized software or backdoors.

Because insiders already have valid credentials, traditional perimeter security tools may fail to detect their activity.

Impact on Security (CIA Model)

  • Confidentiality: Exposure of trade secrets or customer data.
  • Integrity: Manipulation or deletion of organizational records.
  • Availability: Disruption of services or internal systems.

Detection Indicators

  • Accessing systems outside normal working hours.
  • Downloading unusually large amounts of data.
  • Accessing files unrelated to job responsibilities.
  • Repeated privilege escalation attempts.

Mitigation & Defense Strategies

  • Implement the Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP).
  • Use Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
  • Deploy User Behavior Analytics (UBA).
  • Monitor logs with SIEM systems.
  • Conduct regular security awareness training.
